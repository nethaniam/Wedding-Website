OUR love STORY

Our story begins in the city of Indianapolis. We both answered the call to attend Indiana Bible (Bridal) College, not knowing what awaited us. God has a beautiful way of bringing people together—sometimes all it takes is one person moving across the country and another choosing to pursue his calling.


It wasn’t love at first sight, we stumbled into each other’s lives not knowing it would be forever. We first met at breakfast in the school cafeteria. Later, that same cafeteria became the place where we worked side by side, sharing laughs and sneaking leftover frosting from the lids of the pies we prepared for Sunday lunches.


When we both returned for a second year in the fall of 2024, we were voted in as officers for the Urban Missions Club. That role brought us together almost daily, and through planning events, ministry opportunities, and countless meetings, our friendship began to grow into something deeper.


One evening after a Saturday Spanish service, Tavi gave me a ride home, treated me to Wendy’s, and shared his interest in me. With the guidance of spiritual leadership, we chose to take things slow. We didn’t know how it would all work out but we trusted that God was creative and would make a way. It wasn’t until the next semester that we had our very first official date: dinner at Bazbeaux Pizza in downtown Indianapolis, followed by a walk to Moar Tea for boba.


As the months passed, so did our love. We saw one another in every part of Bible college life—our study habits, spiritual disciplines, friendships, and devotion to God.


As my time at IBC came to an end, fear almost took hold of our hearts. But through prayer, we chose to continue loving one another through the distance. On May 10th, we said goodbye at the airport after crying for the entire car ride. It was a bittersweet moment. We were stepping into an uncertain future while trusting that God was still writing our story. We endured a summer apart while Tavi was in Alaska, the growing time difference when he returned to Indianapolis, and all the in-betweens.


In September, Octavio visited California to meet the family. Though it was a short trip, it confirmed once again what we already knew—that we were meant to be. He returned later that year, surprising me on Christmas Eve. We spent the holidays together, and on January 8th, surrounded by his family, Octavio proposed in Malibu. With the ocean and cliffs as our backdrop, I said yes—to the man of my dreams.


And now we are here: still trusting, still believing, and still confident that God is the Author of our story and the Finisher of our faith.
